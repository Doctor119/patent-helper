What is a CPC?

CPC stands for Cooperative Patent Classification. The CPC scheme is a system of classifying patents jointly developed by the European Patent Office (EPO) and United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) to make it easier to categorize patents into their various technological genres. If you patent your invention, the patent examiner will assign it its own CPC.

CPC codes are a lot like taxonomic classifications, in that the beginning part of the code represents its most broad categorization (such as "Textiles" or "Electricity"), and gets more and more specific as you read to the right.
